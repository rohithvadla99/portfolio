Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 18, 2026  ·  Effective immediately

NewsIQ is a personal portfolio project built by Rohith Vadla. This policy explains what data is collected when you use the app and how it is handled.

1. Information We Collect

When you sign in with Google, we receive the following information from Google's OAuth service:

  • Your name and email address
  • Your Google profile picture URL
  • A unique Google account ID

We also collect usage data generated within the app:

  • Articles you mark as read
  • Topic and sentiment preferences you set
  • Company names you add to the watchlist

2. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used solely to provide and improve the NewsIQ experience:

  • To identify your account and maintain your session
  • To personalise your news feed based on your saved preferences
  • To track your reading history and show unread article counts
  • To display your personal reading stats on the dashboard

We do not use your data for advertising, and we do not sell or share your data with any third party.

3. Data Storage

All user data is stored in a SQLite database on the server running the application. Session tokens are stored in the same database and are used to keep you logged in across page reloads.

NewsIQ is hosted on Streamlit Community Cloud. Please refer to Streamlit's Privacy Policy for information about their data handling practices.

4. Google OAuth

NewsIQ uses Google Sign-In for authentication. By signing in, you authorise NewsIQ to receive your basic profile information as described above. We do not request access to your Gmail, Google Drive, or any other Google services beyond your basic profile.

You can revoke NewsIQ's access to your Google account at any time by visiting Google Account Permissions.

5. Data Retention

Your data is retained for as long as you have an active account. You can request deletion of your data by contacting us at the email below. Upon request, we will delete your user record, preferences, read history, and session tokens from our database within 7 days.

6. Children's Privacy

NewsIQ is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any changes. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
